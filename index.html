The counter

Coffee and bakes for the walk between Barnoon and Porthmeor.

Morva Koffi is takeaway only, with a small bench outside and public seating close by near Barnoon car park and Tate St Ives.

  • Locally roasted coffee from Penryn.
  • Small-batch cakes made on the premises.
  • Sandwich and pastry ingredients sourced locally where possible.

Today's shape

A compact menu with serious coffee, Cornish bakes, and fresh savoury options.

Availability changes by season and batch. Ask at the counter about allergens, gluten-free, vegan, and daily specials.

House coffee

Knockout espresso, pulled for the coast path.

Americano, espresso, cortado, macchiato, flat white, latte, cappuccino, and mocha.

  • Flat WhiteDouble short shot with steamed milk in an 8oz cup.
  • Mocha58% Kokoa chocolate with espresso and steamed milk.
  • Cold BrewBatch brew for warmer days and beach walks.
Small batch

Cakes made with local butter, eggs, and proper details.

Daily bakes vary, with gluten-free and vegan-friendly options often available.

  • Guinness CakeDense chocolate cake with cream-cheese frosting.
  • Heavy Cake (Hevva)All-butter Cornish cake adapted from a family recipe.
  • Dark Chocolate Espresso BrownieFairtrade chocolate and Knockout espresso.
Fresh daily

Sausage rolls, croissants, bites, soups, and seasonal bowls.

The savoury counter changes with season and weather, especially out of peak season.

  • Homemade Sausage RollsPastry and Trevaskis Farm pork mince.

Also pouring

Tea, hot chocolate, cold cans, and fairtrade soft drinks.

Expect Cornish Tea, Teapigs, Kokoa Collection chocolate, Lemonaid, Charitea, Fentimans, Cawston Press, Rio, and Coca Cola.

  • Hot ChocolateClassic, smooth, white, and flaked chocolate options.
  • Speciality TeaEarl Grey, lemon and ginger, peppermint, chamomile, green, and rooibos.
  • Cold DrinksJolly's water, organic soft drinks, cans, and children's juice.

Local pantry

The menu is built from local suppliers, not anonymous stock.

Morva Koffi names its sources: Kig and Keus, The Cornish Larder Company, Paul's Bakery, Trewithen Dairy, Colin Carter's, Trevaskis Farm butchery, and Katjacks.

Before you walk down

Small shop, useful answers.

Do you have indoor seating?

No. Morva Koffi is takeaway only, with a small bench outside and public seating near Barnoon car park.

Do you use local products?

Yes. Cakes and sandwiches use locally sourced ingredients where possible, including named Cornish suppliers.

Are opening times fixed?

They can change with heavy rainfall or high winds. The official site and social media carry same-day updates.
