# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Visitors in St Ives who want good takeaway coffee, cake, and savoury bakes near Barnoon, Tate St Ives, and Porthmeor. Many are walking from the car park or the coast path and need fast answers: where it is, whether it is open, what is worth ordering, and where to check weather-related updates.

## Product Purpose

Morva Koffi's site should make the shop feel easy to find, worth the walk, and rooted in local Cornish suppliers. Success means a visitor understands the exact location, knows it is takeaway-only, sees the core menu, and follows the live social channels for weather-sensitive opening updates.

## Brand Personality

Seaside, handmade, practical. The voice should feel independent and local without becoming nostalgic or fussy. The site should carry the same confident hospitality as a small coastal counter with fresh cakes just inside the window.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic coffee-chain branding, AI-style gradient landing pages, oversized app-like cards, beige-only artisan templates, and copy that makes the shop sound like a restaurant with table service. It must not imply indoor seating, table booking, or fixed all-weather opening.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the place: recent exterior photography and Barnoon location details are more important than decorative graphics.
2. Keep the visit frictionless: hours, location, service style, map link, and social updates must be visible without hunting.
3. Make the menu browsable: summarize the rich menu into clear groups and standout items instead of dumping every item at equal weight.
4. Let handmade details carry warmth: use supplier names, batch-made language, and specific bakes rather than generic cafe claims.
5. Respect weather and seasonality: label hours as current website-listed hours and direct users to social updates before a special trip.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic headings, descriptive alt text, reduced-motion support, and no content hidden behind hover-only interactions.
